## Post

I spent more time marketing my product than building it. That felt wrong.

Not because marketing doesn't matter — it absolutely does. But because a solo founder doing keyword research, rewriting CTAs, auditing landing pages, writing tweet threads, and scouring Reddit for distribution channels is a full-time job on top of the actual full-time job.

I kept noticing the same pattern: every marketing task I did fell into one of five buckets — strategy, SEO, copywriting, conversion, and distribution. Five distinct disciplines, each with their own frameworks, each requiring different expertise.

So I built CMO. You paste a URL. Five AI agents analyze your site in parallel — each one a specialist. In about 60 seconds you get a growth strategy that's specific to your product, not generic advice that could apply to any startup.

The part that surprised me most: execution matters more than insight. The tool doesn't just tell you what to do — it generates the content. Tweet threads, blog posts, ad copy. One click.

Three free analyses, no credit card, no subscription. Pay-per-use with USDC when you need more.

What's the one marketing task that eats the most of your time as a founder?
